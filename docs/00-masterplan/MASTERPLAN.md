## Product Identity

Rifqi is not a personal homepage, a static blog, or a standard portfolio website. It is a Digital Headquarters—a unified Personal Operating System. It serves as the owner's single source of truth for knowledge management, personal research, project orchestration, publishing, and future agentic intelligence. 

Rifqi provides a private, structured workspace where the owner thinks, organizes, and designs, alongside a public presentation layer where curated insights, learning, and projects are surfaced to the world.

## Purpose

The purpose of Rifqi is to solve the fragmentation of modern digital existence. Today, an individual's ideas are scattered across ephemeral note-taking apps, local filesystems, public social media networks, and closed project tools. Rifqi consolidates these components into a single, cohesive, long-term repository where information is structured as a semantically connected network of entities.

## Why

This project exists because the lifetime of commercial SaaS platforms is short, whereas the lifetime of an individual's knowledge, ideas, and creations spans decades. By owning the data format, structural architecture, and operational interfaces of a private Digital Headquarters, the owner secures absolute agency over their intellectual property and cognitive history, insulated from vendor lock-in or platform decay.

## Long-Term Vision

Over a ten-year horizon, Rifqi will evolve from a structured repository into an autonomous agentic assistant. By structuring all notes, learnings, projects, and entities with rich relationships and metadata, the system will serve as an externalized cognitive engine. The ten-year target is a symbiotic digital brain capable of executing tasks, conducting deep research, and synthesizing new ideas on behalf of the owner, powered by its highly structured historical data.

## Product Evolution

The system is designed to mature through three distinct stages of evolution:
1. **The Ledger (Years 1–2)**: Establishing the structured schema of entities (Articles, Projects, Learnings, Books, Goals). The focus is on capturing data with absolute fidelity and linking related entities.
2. **The Workspace (Years 3–5)**: Developing active workflows within the private environment, including interactive task management, draft writing, and automatic semantic linking of incoming nodes.
3. **The Agent (Years 6–10)**: Integrating localized semantic indexers and reasoning units that read the connected knowledge base to automate research synthesis, project status monitoring, and publishing schedules.

## Core Domains

To enforce a strict separation of concerns, Rifqi is structured around six core domains:
- **Identity & Authentication**: Managing secure access to the private workspace interfaces.
- **Knowledge & Entities**: The core graph database holding all knowledge assets (Ideas, Learnings, Books).
- **Projects & Tasks**: Execution tracking, managing milestones, actions, and goals.
- **Publishing & Presentation**: Processing internal drafts into clean, public-facing articles and media.
- **Search & Discovery**: High-performance indexing and querying of the entire semantic graph.
- **System Tooling**: Monorepo scripts, deployment assets, and workspace configuration.

## Guiding Principles

- **Owner Data Sovereignty**: Data is stored in open, standard, human-readable formats under direct owner control.
- **Readability Over Speed**: Code and architecture must be clear, clean, and self-documenting to facilitate maintenance across decades.
- **Consistency Over Cleverness**: Simple, repeatable design patterns must be preferred to reduce cognitive overhead during future updates.
- **Semantic Connectivity**: Information is only valuable when linked. Every entity should relate to at least one other entity.

## North Star

The North Star metric is **Connected Knowledge Growth (CKG)**. This measures the net expansion of the semantically connected entities (Learnings, Projects, Articles, Books, Goals) in the Digital Headquarters. A steady increase in connected knowledge indicates that the system is successfully functioning as a compounding cognitive engine rather than a graveyard of isolated files.

## Success Definition

The project is successful if it remains operational, clean, and up-to-date with minimal developer intervention, serving as the daily workspace for the owner, while presenting a polished, high-performance portal to public visitors.

## Final Statement

Rifqi is an investment in cognitive compounding. By systematically recording, organizing, and linking ideas today, we build a foundation that scales in utility and intelligence for the next fifty years.
